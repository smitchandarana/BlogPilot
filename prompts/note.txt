You are writing a personalized LinkedIn connection request note.

Target person:
- Name: {first_name}
- Title: {title}
- Company: {company}
- Shared context (why you're connecting): {shared_context}

Your expertise: {topics}

Rules:
- HARD LIMIT: 300 characters including spaces. Count every character. If over, cut — never cut the reason for connecting.
- Reference something real and specific about them — their role, their company, or the shared context. Generic notes get ignored.
- One concrete reason for connecting that is relevant to THEM, not just your goals.
- No selling. No pitching. No "I'd love to discuss opportunities with you."
- Sound like a real person reaching out, not a script.
- Do NOT add quotation marks around the note — output the note text directly, no wrapping quotes.

BANNED OPENERS — never start with:
- "Hi {first_name}" / "Hey {first_name}"
- "I came across your profile" / "I noticed you work at" / "I'm reaching out because"
- "I saw your post about" / "I stumbled across" / "Your profile caught my eye"

BANNED CLOSINGS — never end with:
- "Looking forward to connecting!" / "Hope to connect!" / "Would love to connect!"
- "Let me know if you're open to it." / "Hope we can chat soon."

BANNED PHRASES anywhere in the note:
- "resonated with me" / "resonated deeply" / "really resonated"
- "I'd love to" / "would love to" / "love to pick your brain"
- "synergy" / "leverage" / "ecosystem" / "game-changer"
- "meaningful connection" / "valuable connection"

STRUCTURE GUIDE (not a template):
- Open with the specific reason you're connecting (their work, a shared topic, their specific role at a specific company)
- State what you bring to the conversation in one short phrase
- Optional: one natural closing phrase — NOT "looking forward to connecting"

CONCRETE EXAMPLES OF GOOD NOTES (study the pattern, don't copy):

Example 1 (role + shared problem):
"Building KPI frameworks for manufacturing ops is exactly the challenge our clients wrestle with. Work in analytics for reporting teams. Would value the perspective."
(163 chars)

Example 2 (context + shared domain):
"Your post on dashboard design for ops teams touched on something our clients face weekly. Analytics background, specialize in BI and reporting. Happy to trade notes."
(168 chars)

Example 3 (company + specific topic):
"Schneider Electric at that scale means your KPI stack has to handle a lot of noise. We work on exactly that problem from the analytics side."
(142 chars)

Note the pattern:
- First sentence: name their specific situation or context directly
- Second sentence: what you bring in one phrase (no "I'd love to" language)
- No closing pleasantry needed

Write ONLY the connection note. No explanation. No quotes. Stay strictly under 300 characters.
